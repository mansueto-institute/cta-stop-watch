StopWatch: Exploring Bus Service in Chicago

Mansueto Logo

This project investigates how the Chicago Transit Authority (CTA) bus service changed after the 2020-2021 COVID-19 pandemic across the city of Chicago. We aim to answer (1) how CTA bus service has changed over time from June 2022 to May 2024 and (2) how bus service varies across Chicago’s community areas. Through this, we reveal which community areas and bus routes have recovered quickest after the decline of service during the COVID-19 pandemic. To answer these questions, we built a novel data set that allows us to know when every bus stopped at every bus stop in the City of Chicago for the past two years.

From this data set, this data set, we computed several metrics that aim to capture the concepts of service reliability and accessibility. Source data is categorized into either actual or scheduled bus service data. The actual bus service data contains 110 million real-time bus locations, representing over 10 million bus trips from June 1, 2022, to May 2024. The Mansueto Stop Watch belongs to the Summer 2024 Mansueto Data Journalism Initiative In addition to this report, we published the comprehensive novel data sets of the CTA bus service, the Bus Report Cards (a platform that allows users to interact with), along with a repository of code and documentation for analysis replication.
